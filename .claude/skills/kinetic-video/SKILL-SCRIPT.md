# Script Writing Guide

Read this before writing any script. These rules come from real iterations, not theory.

---

## 1. Write the Through-Line First

One sentence. Before a single word of script:

> "What will the viewer believe after watching that they didn't believe before?"

Cannot write it? You have a topic, not a script. Stop and think harder.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Fiber causes bloating" → observation, not a flip
- ✅ "Bloating after fiber means your gut is alive — dead guts don't bloat"
- ❌ "High-fiber Japanese foods" → topic
- ✅ "Japan's longevity secret isn't sushi — it's three foods nobody orders"

---

## 2. The BUT/THEREFORE Test

Between every two lines, insert a connector.
- "and then" → line is broken, cut or rewrite
- "but" or "therefore" → line works

**FiberGas — every line earns its place:**
```
You added fiber to your diet.
→ THEREFORE that's the reason you're bloated.    ✅
Your gut just started working twice as hard.
→ THEREFORE DEAD GUTS DON'T BLOAT.               ✅ (WTF flip)
The bacteria weren't ready for it.
→ THEREFORE hit them slowly.                     ✅
Don't blame the broccoli.
→ THEREFORE [implicit: blame the pace, not the food] ✅
That apple in your fridge — give it a shot.
→ THEREFORE four weeks. Then decide.             ✅
```

**FiberJapan — list that works:**
```
Japan. Longest lives on the planet.
→ THEREFORE their secret is worth knowing.
Not a pill. Not a diet plan. Three foods.
→ THEREFORE [here are the three]:
  Edamame / Natto / Burdock root         ← consistent structure: name + number + unit
→ BUT most people eat sashimi — zero fiber.  ✅ (pivot that justifies the list)
→ THEREFORE swap one thing. Every single day. ✅
```
A list works when: every item has the same structure AND there's a pivot after it that explains why it matters.

---

## 3. The WTF Moment

Every script needs one sentence that stops the scroll. The through-line delivered as a surprise.

**FiberGas:** "DEAD GUTS DON'T BLOAT." — reframes bloating from problem to proof of life.
**FiberJapan:** "Most people eat sashimi. Zero fiber." — the famous healthy food is the counterexample.

How to find it: what does the viewer currently believe? What's the exact opposite that's also true?

**This is the CAPS word(s) in the script.** CAPS → hero orange 280px in the video. Use deliberately. One moment per video.

---

## 4. What Fails — Real Examples

These were rejected during FiberGas production:

| What was written | Why it failed | Fix |
|-----------------|---------------|-----|
| "You added fiber to your diet" as opening | No hook — states the obvious, no pattern interrupt | Open with the consequence: "If you feel bloated after every healthy meal — watch this." |
| "So you quit fiber" | "Quit fiber" makes no sense. Unnatural. No connection to previous line | "So you quit your diet" |
| "Your gut went into turbo mode" | Too hype, sounds like a fitness influencer, not David Miller | "Your gut just started working twice as hard." |
| "You'll come back to thank me" | Promise. Hype. Opposite of David Miller | "Four weeks. Then decide." |
| Two-word connection lines | No logic, felt disconnected | Add an explicit bridge: "That's the reason." |

## 5. Myth-Bust Structure

Best format for health and nutrition content:

```
[HOOK — 0-3 seconds]
  Pattern interrupt. Curiosity gap.
  Start with: surprising fact, place name, number, direct challenge.
  NEVER: "Today I'm going to..." / "In this video..." / "Did you know..."

[AGITATE — 3-10 seconds]
  Specific behavior, not vague generality.
  "Most people [specific behavior]" not "people often"
  Build toward the WTF moment.

[WTF MOMENT — 1-2 seconds]
  The through-line delivered. One sentence.
  Write in CAPS → becomes hero orange in video.

[TRUTH + SPECIFICS — 15-25 seconds]
  Numbers: "8 grams" not "high in fiber"
  Names: "lentils" not "legumes"
  If list: consistent structure per item + pivot after list

[CLOSE — 5-8 seconds]
  One action. Not a summary.
  Then fixed outro: "Recipes at dailylifehacks.com / Free. Every week."
```

---

## 6. Sentence-Level Rules

**Specific beats general:**
```
❌ "This food is high in fiber."
✅ "Eighteen grams. More than half your daily target."
```

**Second person:**
```
❌ "Most people don't get enough fiber."
✅ "You're probably getting half the fiber you need."
```

**Vary sentence length:**
```
❌ "Avocado is a great fat. It's good for you. It fills you up."
✅ "Avocado. Half on toast. That's it."
```
Short = emphasis. Long = context. Never two consecutive sentences the same length.

**Contractions always:**
it's / don't / can't / won't / they're / we've / here's / that's

---

## 7. David Miller Voice

Anti-hype. Dry. Practical. Friend who knows nutrition — not a doctor, not an ad.

**Banned words:** Furthermore, Moreover, In conclusion, Delve into, Dive into, It's important to note, It's worth noting, In today's world, Unlock, Elevate, Game-changer, Revolutionize, Mouthwatering

**Banned endings:** "Enjoy!", "Happy eating!", "Give it a try!", "You won't regret it!", "Your gut will thank you!"

**No medical claims:** "cures", "treats", "heals", "relieves", "prevents", "fights"
Use instead: "may support", "could help", "might improve"

**No detox language:** "detox", "cleanses your body", "reset your system"

---

## 8. Checklist Before Showing Script

```
[ ] Wrote through-line in one sentence before starting
[ ] BUT/THEREFORE: every two lines connect
[ ] Hook ≤3 seconds, doesn't start with "Today" / "In this video"
[ ] One clear WTF moment, written in CAPS
[ ] Every number specific: "8 grams" not "high in fiber"
[ ] Every name specific: "lentils" not "vegetables"
[ ] No two consecutive sentences the same length
[ ] Second person only: "you", not "people" / "most of us"
[ ] URL in script: "dailylifehacks.com" (no dashes)
[ ] Outro line: "Recipes at dailylifehacks.com / Free. Every week."
[ ] No banned words, no medical claims
[ ] Length: 130-160 words ≈ 40 seconds at Roger pace
```
