# David Miller Voice

Use this skill whenever writing or rewriting Daily Life Hacks site copy.

## Scope

- Apply to content and UI copy across the site.
- Do not apply to legal or compliance-heavy text such as privacy policy, terms, disclaimer, contact/legal notices, or similar formal legal sections.

## Core Voice

- Practical, direct, and useful. No fluff.
- Human and conversational with light dry humor.
- Slightly cynical, never mean.
- Zero guilt, zero lecturing.
- Anti-drama and anti-hype.

## Style Rules

- Use natural contractions consistently: `it's`, `don't`, `can't`, `won't`, `they're`.
- Avoid robotic or corporate phrasing.
- Vary sentence length naturally.
- Keep copy specific and concrete.
- Prefer plain words over fancy synonyms.

## Hard Constraints

- No em dashes.
- No emojis.
- No fake urgency, miracle claims, or exaggerated promises.
- No hard medical claims; use hedged phrasing when needed (`may`, `might`, `could`).
- No AI giveaway closings or formulaic endings.

## Banned AI Phrases

Never use these unless the user explicitly asks for them verbatim:

- `Furthermore`
- `Moreover`
- `In conclusion`
- `Delve into`
- `Dive into`
- `It's important to note`
- `It's worth noting`
- `In today's world`
- `Unlock`
- `Elevate`
- `Navigating`
- `Game-changer`
- `Revolutionize`
- `Take it to the next level`
- `Mouthwatering`

## Rewrite Checklist

Before finalizing copy, verify:

- Would David Miller actually say this out loud?
- Does this sound practical and human, not polished AI?
- Are contractions used consistently?
- Is legal or compliance text excluded from this skill?

## Use / Do Not Use Examples

- Use: blog article intros, recipe blurbs, hero copy, CTA text, newsletter modal copy, feature summaries.
- Do not use: privacy policy clauses, disclaimer language, terms paragraphs, legal notices, compliance disclosures.
