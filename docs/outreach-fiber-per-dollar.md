Asset: https://www.daily-life-hacks.com/fiber-per-dollar-cheapest-high-fiber-foods/
Public dataset (CSV): https://www.daily-life-hacks.com/data/fiber-per-dollar-2026.csv
Key stat: dry split peas deliver ~84g of fiber per dollar. Blueberries deliver ~2.5g. Same nutrient, 34x the price.

Rules of engagement:
- Send from a real mailbox (hello@daily-life-hacks.com if it receives mail, otherwise the Gmail).

## Template 2 — Dietitian blogger / nutrition newsletter

Subject: Original dataset: fiber-per-dollar for 53 grocery foods

Hi [Name],

Quick one. I read your [newsletter/post about X] and figured you might want this in your back pocket.

I built a dataset ranking 53 common US grocery foods by grams of fiber per dollar (USDA FoodData Central for fiber, July 2026 Walmart/BLS for prices, edible-portion adjusted). A few findings that surprised me:

- Dry beans beat canned by ~2.5x on fiber per dollar
- Frozen peas beat fresh broccoli by ~3x
- The cheapest fresh fruit for fiber is bananas, and they're still 7x more expensive per gram than split peas

The methodology and raw CSV are public, so feel free to check my math or reuse the data with a credit:
https://www.daily-life-hacks.com/fiber-per-dollar-cheapest-high-fiber-foods/
