"How much protein do I need?" is one of those questions that gets a different answer depending on who you ask. Fitness influencers throw out numbers that sound like they're for bodybuilders. Old-school guidelines can feel low. The truth is somewhere in the middle, and it depends on your size, how active you are, and what you're trying to do.

## The Basic Range

A common starting point is about 0.36 grams of protein per pound of body weight. That's the minimum recommended for most adults to avoid deficiency. For a 150-pound person, that's around 54 grams per day. For many people, that's enough to stay healthy. If you're more active, older, or trying to build or keep muscle, research suggests that going higher may help. A range of 0.5 to 0.8 grams per pound is often cited for people who exercise regularly or want to preserve muscle as they age. That same 150-pound person might aim for 75 to 120 grams per day in that case.

## Why It's Not One Number

Your body uses protein for way more than building biceps. It's involved in immune function, hormones, enzymes, and basic repair. If you sit at a desk all day and don't lift weights, you probably don't need the upper end of the range. If you're training hard or in your 50s and want to keep strength, a bit more protein might be useful. "Might" is the word. We're not saying protein may support anything or guarantees results; we're saying the evidence points to a range that could support your goals.

## Hitting Your Target Without Obsessing

You don't need an app for every meal. A palm-sized portion of chicken, fish, or meat at dinner, some Greek yogurt or eggs at breakfast, and maybe beans or lentils in a lunch dish gets many people into a reasonable range. If you're vegetarian or vegan, combining grains and legumes over the day can give you enough of the building blocks your body needs. The goal is to make it sustainable, not to hit a perfect number every single day.

## When to Take It Seriously (And When Not To)

Most people in the U.S. get enough protein without thinking about it. If you're cutting calories a lot, recovering from illness, or you're older and not eating much, paying a little attention to protein could help. If you're healthy, active, and eating a mix of foods, you're probably fine. The hype around massive protein intakes is just that. A moderate amount, spread across the day, is usually enough.
