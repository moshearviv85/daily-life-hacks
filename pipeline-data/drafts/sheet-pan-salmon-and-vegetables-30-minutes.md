ingredients:
  - 4 (5–6 oz) skin-on salmon fillets
  - 1 lb broccoli florets
  - 1 medium zucchini, cut into 1/2-inch half-moons
  - 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  - 1/2 tsp dried dill or thyme
  - Salt and black pepper to taste
  - Lemon wedges for serving
steps:
  - Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a large sheet pan with parchment or lightly oil it.
  - Toss broccoli, zucchini, and tomatoes with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, half
    the dill, salt, and pepper. Spread on the pan and roast 10 minutes.
  - Push vegetables to the sides. Place salmon fillets in the center, skin side down.
    Drizzle with remaining 1 tbsp olive oil and sprinkle with remaining dill, salt,
    and pepper.
  - Roast 10–12 minutes until salmon flakes easily with a fork and veggies are tender
    and lightly charred. Serve with lemon wedges.

Sheet pan dinners are popular for a reason. You put everything on one tray, stick it in the oven, and when the timer goes off you have a full meal and one pan to wash.

This sheet pan salmon and vegetables recipe is built for a busy night. The vegetables go in first for a few minutes, then the salmon joins them. In about 30 minutes total you get flaky salmon and roasted veggies without juggling multiple pans or recipes.

## Why This Combo Works

Salmon cooks quickly and doesn't need much besides oil, salt, and a little herb. Broccoli, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes roast well at the same high heat and don't need a lot of babysitting. Starting the veggies first gives them a head start so they're done when the salmon is. If you like a bit more char, you can broil for a minute at the end, but it's not required.

## Picking Salmon and Veggies

Skin-on fillets hold together better on the sheet pan; you can leave the skin on and eat around it or remove it after cooking. Aim for similar thickness so everything finishes at once. You can swap in asparagus or green beans for some of the broccoli if you prefer. Just keep the pieces similar in size so they roast evenly.

## Keeping It Simple

There's no sauce to make unless you want one. A squeeze of lemon is enough for most people. If you have a little Greek yogurt or Dijon in the fridge, a quick dollop on the side works too. Leftover salmon and veggies keep in the fridge for a day or two and reheat gently so the fish doesn't dry out. One pan, one timer, one less thing to figure out at the end of the day.
