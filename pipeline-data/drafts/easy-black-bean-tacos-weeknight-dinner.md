Tacos don't have to mean ground beef and a long ingredient list. These easy black bean tacos are built for a weeknight: open a couple of cans, warm the beans with a few spices, and put out tortillas and toppings. Dinner is done in about 15 minutes.

## Why Black Beans in Tacos

Black beans are cheap, shelf-stable, and full of protein and fiber. Mashing some of them in the pan gives you a mix that's thick enough to stay in the tortilla but still has texture. Cumin and chili powder do the heavy lifting for flavor. You're not making refried beans from scratch; you're just heating and seasoning. That's the point.

## Keeping It Flexible

You can use corn or flour tortillas, whatever you have. Toppings are up to you: shredded cabbage or lettuce, salsa, lime, cilantro, maybe a little avocado or crema. If you want to add cheese or pickled onions, go for it. The base is the same; everyone can build the taco they want.

## Making It a Meal

A side of rice or a simple slaw rounds it out if you're hungry. The beans keep in the fridge for a few days, so you can warm a portion and make tacos again without starting over. No fancy techniques, no all-day simmering. Just a reliable dinner that doesn't ask much of you.
