ingredients:
  - 1 lb ground turkey (93% lean)
  - 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  - 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (or 1 tsp dried)
  - 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  - 1/4 tsp salt
  - 1/4 tsp black pepper
  - 1 tbsp olive oil for cooking
steps:
  - In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, breadcrumbs, egg, garlic, parsley, oregano,
    salt, and pepper. Mix with your hands until just combined; don't overmix or meatballs
    can get tough.
  - Shape into 20–24 meatballs (about 1.5 tbsp each) and place on a plate.
  - Heat olive oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add meatballs in
    a single layer (work in batches if needed) and cook 8–10 minutes, turning occasionally,
    until golden and cooked through (165°F in the center).
  - Serve right away over pasta or with a side, or let cool and store in airtight
    containers in the fridge up to 4 days or freeze up to 3 months.

Turkey meatballs are one of those things that sound "healthy" and sometimes taste like it in a bad way. These don't. They're moist, seasoned simply, and built for making ahead.

This healthy turkey meatballs meal prep recipe gives you a big batch in about 35 minutes. You can eat them the same night with marinara and pasta, pack them for lunch with a grain and veggies, or drop them into soup later in the week.

## Why Turkey Works for Meal Prep

Ground turkey is lean, so it doesn't release a lot of grease when you cook it. A little breadcrumb and one egg keep the meatballs from drying out. The flavor comes from garlic, parsley, and oregano. Nothing fancy, but it holds up well in the fridge and freezer so you can cook once and use the batch a few different ways.

## Shaping and Cooking

Wet your hands slightly when you roll the meatballs so the mix doesn't stick. Keep them all about the same size so they cook evenly. If your skillet is small, cook in two batches rather than crowding the pan. They're done when they're golden on the outside and register 165°F in the center. A few minutes of rest and they're ready to eat or to cool and pack away.

## How to Use Them

Toss with marinara and spaghetti, add to a sub roll with a little sauce and cheese, or serve over rice or quinoa with steamed broccoli. They reheat well in the microwave or in a pan with a splash of water or sauce. If you freeze them, thaw in the fridge overnight before reheating. One batch, several meals, and you're not eating the same exact plate every day.
