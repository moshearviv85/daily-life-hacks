For a long time, fat was the villain. Now we know that the type of fat and how much you eat matter more than cutting it out. Healthy fats can help you feel full, support your cells, and make food taste good. This list is about foods that fit into a normal diet without turning every meal into a fat-focused project.

## Oils for Cooking and Dressings

Olive oil is the one most people know: good for dressings, roasting, and light sauteing. It's mostly monounsaturated fat, which is the kind that's often linked to heart health in the context of a balanced diet. Avocado oil has a higher smoke point, so it works well for higher-heat cooking. For dressings and drizzling, a little sesame or walnut oil can add flavor. You don't need a cabinet full of bottles; one or two good all-purpose oils are enough to start.

## Nuts and Seeds

Almonds, walnuts, pecans, and peanuts are easy to add to meals or eat as a snack. A small handful (about an ounce) gives you a dose of fat, some protein, and fiber. Chia seeds, flaxseeds, and pumpkin seeds are another way to get healthy fats; you can stir them into yogurt, oatmeal, or smoothies. Just keep portions reasonable since nuts and seeds are calorie-dense.

## Avocado

Avocado is mostly fat, and it's the monounsaturated kind. You can slice it on toast, add it to salads, blend it into smoothies, or use it as a spread. It doesn't fix anything by itself, but it's a versatile way to add fat and creaminess to everyday meals.

## Fatty Fish

Salmon, mackerel, sardines, and herring are rich in omega-3 fats, which are often talked about for heart and brain health. The research is still evolving, but including fatty fish a couple of times a week is a common recommendation. Canned salmon and sardines are cheap and don't require cooking if you're short on time.

## Whole Foods Over Supplements

Getting fats from food means you also get other nutrients. A piece of fish gives you protein; nuts give you fiber. Pill form isn't bad if a doctor suggests it, but for most people, a handful of nuts, some olive oil on a salad, and fish once or twice a week is a simple way to include healthy fats without overthinking it.
