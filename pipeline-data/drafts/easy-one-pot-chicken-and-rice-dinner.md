One pot means one pan to wash. That's the whole pitch.

This easy one-pot chicken and rice dinner is the kind of meal you can make on a Tuesday when you're tired and don't want to think. You brown some chicken, add rice and broth, and let it simmer. No fancy techniques, no separate pots for the rice. Everything cooks together and comes out tender and flavorful.

## Why One-Pot Works Here

Cooking the rice in the same pan as the chicken does two things: the rice soaks up the savory drippings, and you skip the usual pile of dishes. Long-grain white rice holds up well and won't turn to mush if you keep the heat low and the lid on. Chicken thighs stay juicier than breasts in this kind of dish, so they're a better fit for a relaxed weeknight.

## What You Need (No Surprises)

You'll need a deep skillet or a Dutch oven with a tight-fitting lid. If you don't have one, a large pot works too. The rest is standard stuff: chicken thighs, onion, garlic, rice, broth, and a couple of pantry spices. Frozen peas at the end are optional but add a bit of color and fiber without extra work.

## Tips for Getting It Right

Don't skip rinsing the rice; it keeps the grains from getting too sticky. When you add the broth, make sure it's at a full boil before you turn the heat down and cover. That way the rice cooks evenly. If the rice is still a bit wet when the timer goes off, leave it covered off the heat for another 5 minutes and it'll absorb the rest.

Leftovers keep well in the fridge for a few days and reheat nicely with a splash of water or broth. You've got a real dinner with minimal cleanup, and that's the point.
