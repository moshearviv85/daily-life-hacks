If you walk into a major kitchen supply store, you will be overwhelmed by an absolute wall of completely unnecessary plastic junk. There are machines that exclusively chop garlic, terrifying contraptions designed solely to core apples, and weirdly specific tongs just for pulling toast out of the toaster. 

These "unitaskers" only perform one highly specific job. After you use them once, they get shoved into the back of a deep drawer where they jam everything up until you eventually donate them three years later. Buying unitaskers is a massive waste of both money and physical space in your kitchen. 

However, there is a very small, highly specific list of gadgets that actually earn their keep. Finding the right kitchen tools that save time and money is about identifying the items that perform multiple tasks incredibly well, drastically speeding up your prep work so you are more likely to actually cook instead of ordering takeout. 

Here are the four ultimate workhorses that belong in absolutely every kitchen. 

## 1. The Microplane Grater

If I could only bring one gadget with me to a deserted island, it would be a microplane. It looks like a long, skinny wood file, and it is arguably the most versatile flavor-enhancing tool in existence. 

You do not need a terrifyingly huge box grater, and you definitely do not need a garlic press (which are impossible to clean anyway). A microplane does it all instantly. You use it to grate a hard wedge of parmesan cheese into perfect fluffy clouds over pasta. You use it to instantly turn a clove of garlic into a smooth paste directly into a hot pan. Most importantly, you use it to "zest" lemons and limes, taking just the bright, incredibly flavorful yellow skin off without hitting the bitter white pith underneath. Zest instantly upgrades everything from chicken marinades to salad dressings. 

## 2. A Heavy-Duty Citrus Squeezer

We all know we are supposed to use fresh lemon or lime juice to make our food taste better, but squeezing them by hand is deeply annoying. You get sticky juice all over your fingers, the seeds fall directly into your food, and you honestly leave half the juice inside the fruit anyway because you cannot physically squeeze it hard enough. 

A metal, enameled citrus squeezer (the kind that looks like a handheld clam shell) completely eliminates this problem. You cut the lemon in half, put it face down in the squeezer, and press the handles together. In exactly three seconds, it extracts every single drop of juice while perfectly catching all the seeds. It makes making fresh salad dressings or squeezing lime over tacos a total breeze. 

## 3. The Digital Meat Thermometer

This is the tool that saves you massive amounts of money by completely preventing ruined dinners. 

Meat is expensive. If you buy a beautiful steak or a thick pork chop and you accidentally overcook it until it is dry and gray, it is a tragedy. Most people guess if meat is done by cutting it open while it is in the pan, which lets all the juices immediately drain out, ruining the texture. 

A digital instant-read thermometer completely removes the anxiety of cooking meat. You just wait until the chicken hits exactly 165 degrees Fahrenheit, pull it off the heat, and you know with absolute scientific certainty that it is perfectly safe to eat and perfectly juicy. Stop guessing and start measuring. 

## 4. The Bench Scraper

This is a totally unassuming rectangle of metal with a handle along one edge. It is completely obscure to most home cooks, but professional chefs use them constantly. 

When you spend ten minutes finely dicing an onion or chopping a pile of carrots, picking them up with your hands or trying to scrape them off the cutting board with the delicate edge of your expensive chef's knife is terrible. The knife will go dull incredibly fast. 

A bench scraper allows you to instantly scoop up a massive mound of chopped vegetables in one single motion to transfer them into a hot pan. Furthermore, when you are done cooking, you use that flat metal edge to aggressively scrape all the sticky, stuck-on food bits right off your counter or cutting board into the trash. It cuts your cleanup time entirely in half. 

Focusing on these few kitchen tools that save time and money will completely streamline your cooking process. Stop buying the gimmick gadgets and invest a few dollars in the tools the professionals actually use.
