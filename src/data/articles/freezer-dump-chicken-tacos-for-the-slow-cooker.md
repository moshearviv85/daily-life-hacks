ingredients:
  - 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  - 1 (15 oz) can black beans, rinsed and drained
  - 1 (15 oz) can corn, drained
  - 1 (10 oz) can diced tomatoes with green chilies (Rotel), undrained
  - 1 (8 oz) can tomato sauce
  - 1 packet (1 oz) taco seasoning mix
  - 1/2 cup chicken broth
steps:
  - Place chicken breasts or thighs at the bottom of a large freezer-safe bag.
  - Add rinsed black beans, drained corn, undrained diced tomatoes with green chilies, tomato sauce, taco seasoning, and chicken broth to the bag.
  - Seal the bag, removing as much air as possible, and gently massage to combine ingredients and distribute the seasoning.
  - Lay the bag flat in your freezer. Freeze for up to 3 months.
  - When ready to cook, remove the frozen block from the freezer bag and place it directly into your slow cooker.
  - Cook on LOW for 6-8 hours or on HIGH for 3-4 hours, or until the chicken is cooked through and easily shreds.
  - Once cooked, remove the chicken to a cutting board and shred it using two forks.
  - Return the shredded chicken to the slow cooker and stir it into the sauce.
  - Serve hot in tortillas or taco shells with your favorite toppings.

There are days when the thought of deciding what's for dinner, let alone making it, feels like scaling Everest in flip-flops. You know the ones. You walk in the door, everyone's hungry, and suddenly a frozen pizza starts looking like a gourmet meal. That's exactly why I love a good freezer dump meal. It's the ultimate "future you" gift.

This recipe for slow cooker chicken tacos is one of those gifts. You spend maybe ten minutes on a weekend or a quieter evening, toss everything into a bag, and forget about it. Later, when the chaos of a Tuesday evening hits, you pull out a ready-to-cook meal from the freezer, dump it in the slow cooker, and walk away. A few hours later, you've got flavorful, shredded chicken tacos with minimal effort. It's a win, plain and simple.

### Why a Freezer Dump Meal is Your Weeknight Hero

Let's be honest, we're all looking for shortcuts that don't taste like shortcuts. This recipe delivers. The beauty of a freezer dump meal is that all the heavy lifting – the chopping, the measuring, the combining – happens once. When it's time to cook, there's no thawing required. You just open the bag, slide the frozen block into your slow cooker, and let the magic happen. The ingredients meld together as they cook, creating a deeply flavored sauce that coats the shredded chicken perfectly. It's the kind of cooking that feels like cheating, but tastes like you put in hours.

### The Chicken: Breast or Thigh?

For this recipe, you can go either way. Boneless, skinless chicken breasts are lean and shred beautifully, making them a solid choice. If you're after a bit more flavor and a slightly juicier result, boneless, skinless chicken thighs are your ticket. They tend to hold up a little better over longer cooking times without drying out. I usually grab whatever's on sale, because honestly, with all the seasonings and sauce, it's going to be good regardless. Just make sure it's boneless and skinless for easy shredding later.

### Assembling Your Freezer Bag: A Few Tips

Getting this meal into the freezer bag correctly is key for both space-saving and even cooking. First, lay your chicken flat at the bottom of the bag. This helps it freeze more evenly and allows the sauce ingredients to surround it. Then, layer in your beans, corn, tomatoes, sauce, seasoning, and broth. Do not worry about mixing it too much yet. Once everything is in, seal the bag almost all the way, leaving a small gap. Squeeze out as much air as you can, then seal it completely. Gently massage the bag to distribute the seasoning and ensure the chicken is coated. Lay the bag flat on a baking sheet or directly in your freezer until it's solid. This flat shape means it stacks easily and thaws (in the slow cooker, that is) more efficiently.

### Cooking Day: From Freezer to Fork

When the day comes to cook, you do not need to do anything but grab the frozen bag. Take the block out of the bag and pop it straight into your slow cooker. Set it on LOW for 6-8 hours or HIGH for 3-4 hours. The slower cook time on low generally results in more tender chicken, but high works in a pinch if you are short on time. You will know it is ready when the chicken is fall-apart tender and shreds easily with two forks. Do not skip the shredding step; it helps the chicken absorb all that delicious sauce.

### Serving Suggestions and Variations

Once your chicken is shredded and mixed back into the sauce, you are ready to eat. Spoon the mixture into warm tortillas or crispy taco shells. Then, it is time for the fun part: toppings. Shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, a dollop of sour cream or plain Greek yogurt, shredded cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese, salsa, guacamole, or even a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice all work wonderfully.

Want to mix it up? You could serve this over rice for a taco bowl, or even as a filling for quesadillas. If you like a little extra heat, throw in a chopped jalapeño or a pinch of cayenne pepper when you are assembling the freezer bag. For a smoky flavor, add a teaspoon of smoked paprika. This recipe is pretty forgiving, so feel free to experiment with what you have on hand. It is all about making your life a little easier, one delicious bite at a time.
